High Noon

My first experience with any kind of game development. High Noon is a tabletop board game that pits four players in a survival scenario where they must collect resources and build shelter. The project was done as part of Shanghai American School's Innovation Institute project-based learning program, and is intended to analyze the tendencies of people to collaborate or compete with others when placed in situations where both outcomes have benefits.

This project was a group effort alongside classmates Luca Lee, Leonard Lee, and Tori Li. With respect to team responsibilities, I structured the core gameplay loop and came up with most of the game's mechanics.

As one can tell, the game and its content are "heavily inspired" by a certain movement shooter that came out during that time period (please don't sue any of us Blizzard we really liked Overwatch)

Transcription of text:

A group of people wake up in a forest at 6 AM. A note is left for them which reads: "In 6 hours you are all going to die, unless you can build a shelter and be under it by that time. But can you make it?" The note ends. As one of the people, you can choose to work with the others or work against them, The Choice is yours.

The Setup:

1. The Board is set up with one area on top, one area on the bottom, and one in the middle. The forest area must be at the middle.

2. The chance card pile is shuffled and placed next to the board.

3. Each player picks a random character car and recieve the player piece for that card.

4. After recieving the piece, place it in the middle of the Forest.

5. The order of turns is determined by rolling the 8-sided die. The player who rolled the highest goes first. If a tie is made between players, the players roll the die again.

The Rules:

1. At the start of a turn, the player rolls to determine how much energy they have for that turn.

2. At the end of each turn, the player draws a card from the Chance pile. If it is an event, it must be used immediately

3. The die used will change as the game progresses, from a 4-sided die to a 6-sided die, and an 8-sided die.

4. Players may not move diagonally

Mechanics: Energy:

Energy is rolled at the start of every turn. It is used when the player wants to perform an action.

Actions that use Energy

Movement: 1 Energy per space

Placing a Wood Block: 1 Energy

Placing a Stone Block: 1 Energy

Placing a Steel Block: 1 Energy

Movement: 1 Energy per space

Slimland

What started as a personal project for working with Unity eventually became a small submission to my first game jam. Slimland is a platformer game where you play as a happy blue slime. This was my first solo game project, done with the intent of developing skills in all aspects of game development. As such, all assets in this project are self-made. A short version consisting of two levels was submitted to Devtober 2020, and I have put development for it on hiatus for now to focus on improving other skills

Project Mindswipe

The big final project for my Game Development I course. Project Mindswipe is a stealth game which has players sequentially break into various locations to steal something, and then break out of those same locations after the item is taken. Project Mindswipe is my first experience with "proper" video game development, as we created, developed, and communicated the game to our professor and graders over a specified period of time, before concluding with a presentation.

This project was a group effort alongside Drake Miller, Lance Davis, Ram Venkatesh, Dana Ravago and Vinnie Colosimo, and was created in GameMaker Studio 2. With respect to team responsibilities, I designed each level's rooms and room mechanics, and wrote the random level generation algorithm to create levels with those rooms.

Project Mindswipe was created in GameMaker Studio 2 and can be downloaded and played here

Just Ice Served

Just Ice Served is a 2D infinite runner that tells the story of a snowball throwing vigilante set on ridding their neighborhood of the local mob. The game includes a Story mode for players that wish to experience the tale firsthand, and an Endless mode for players that wish to test their limits.

Just Ice Served was created in Unity for itch.io's 2-Button Jam and was a partner effort with Kyle Liang (Georgia Institute of Technology). Background music for the game was created by Stefan DeBates (Georgia Institute of Technology) and used with permission. I created all the art for the game in Photoshop, and programmed scripts relating to player controls, enemy AI, and cutscene UI. Just Ice Served can be downloaded and played here

Stained in ScARlet

The project for the AR side of Immersive Creation Studio's Summer 2021 AR/VR Guild. Stained in ScARlet is a mystery-themed, investigation-based AR game that has the player look for clues across multiple short narratives and locations.

This project was a group effort alongside fellow guild members Aira San Agustin, Zoe Purcell, and Julianna Piechowicz, and was created in Unity. I programmed many of the functional scripts for Stained in ScARlet, and it was my first time developing for and programming around an AR environment.

Shoot the Aliens

Shoot the Aliens is a 3D first-person style shooter. Players aim to get a high score as they fend off the alien horde.

This project was my final project for ASU's Game Engine Development course, and utilizes a Monogame-based game engine library created by me during the course. Models and terrain map used in the game were reused from course assignments, while the alien model was a free asset found online. Background music for the game was created by Stefan DeBates (Georgia Institute of Technology) and used with permission.
